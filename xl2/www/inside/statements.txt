<!--Basic XL statements-->

{@xl} (more precisely, {concept "xl012" "XL1"} features the basic
statement expected for most imperative programming languages.

<B>Declarations</B> in general behave like {@C++} declarations,
including the ability to declare an initialization or default value,
and the existence of {concept "ctors" "constructors and destructors"}.
There are {@generic} entities. Declarations can always be declared
using the {tt ":"} {concept "operators" "operator"}, but special
forms exist for types, {example "function" "functions"} and a few
other kinds of entities.
